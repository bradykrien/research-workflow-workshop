# Literature Monitoring
{: .fs-8 }

Research moves fast. Stay current with new research in your field using BrowZine and Google Scholar Alerts.
{: .fs-5 .fw-300 }

---

## What is It Good For?

A new article came out six months ago that's directly relevant to your dissertation chapter, and you only found out about it from a footnote in someone else's paper. Literature monitoring tools send new publications *to you* so you don't have to remember to go looking.

**Where it fits in the workflow:** Discover

---

## Two tools, one goal

| | BrowZine | Google Scholar Alerts |
|:--|:---------|:---------------------|
| **What it does** | Browse and follow journals; get table-of-contents alerts | Get email alerts for new work by specific authors or on specific topics |
| **Best for** | Following the key journals in your field | Tracking specific people, topics, or your own citation count |
| **Cost** | Free through your institution | Free |
| **Setup time** | ~10 minutes | ~10 minutes |

They complement each other well. BrowZine is journal-centric ("tell me when *American Historical Review* publishes something new"). Google Scholar is topic- and author-centric ("tell me when someone publishes on *digital labor in the Progressive Era*").

---

## Path A: BrowZine (20 min)

### 1. Access BrowZine (3 min)

- Go to [browzine.com](https://browzine.com/) or search your library's website for "BrowZine"
- Sign in through your institution — BrowZine uses your library's subscriptions, so you need to authenticate
- You can also download the BrowZine app for iOS or Android

### 2. Find your journals (10 min)

- Browse by subject area or search for specific journal titles
- When you find a journal you want to follow, click the "+" or "Add to My Bookshelf" button

**Exercise:** Think of 5 journals that are central to your field. Search for each one and add it to your bookshelf.

### 3. Set up alerts (5 min)

- Once you've built your bookshelf, BrowZine will notify you when new issues are published
- Check your notification settings — you can get email alerts or just check the app/site periodically
- BrowZine also lets you save individual articles and link out to your library's full-text access

### 4. Explore the reading experience (2 min)

- Tap into a journal on your bookshelf to see recent issues
- BrowZine provides a visual table of contents that's often easier to browse than the journal's own website

## Path B: Google Scholar Alerts (20 min)

### 1. Set up your Google Scholar profile (5 min)

- Go to [scholar.google.com](https://scholar.google.com/)
- Click "My Profile" (you'll need a Google account)
- Fill in your name, affiliation, and areas of interest
- Add your own publications if you have any (Scholar may have already found some)
- Under "Follow new articles" and "Follow new citations," make sure email notifications are turned on

### 2. Create topic alerts (10 min)

- On the Google Scholar homepage, run a search for a topic important to your research
- At the bottom left of the results page, click the **envelope icon** or "Create Alert"
- Enter your email and confirm
- Scholar will email you when new papers match that search

**Tips for good alerts:**
- Be specific enough to avoid noise: "digital labor Progressive Era" will serve you better than "labor history"
- Use quotes for exact phrases: `"spatial humanities"`
- Create 3–5 alerts to start — you can always adjust later

**Exercise:** Create 3 alerts — one broad (your subfield), one narrow (your specific topic), and one for a key author or scholar whose work you always want to see.

### 3. Follow specific authors (5 min)

- Search for scholars whose work matters to your research
- Visit their Google Scholar profile
- Click "Follow" — you'll get an email when they publish new work or when their work gets cited

**Exercise:** Follow 3–5 scholars in your area.

## Combining both tools

A strong monitoring setup might look like:
- **BrowZine** for the 5–8 journals you consider "must-read"
- **Google Scholar alerts** for 3–5 specific topics and 5–10 key scholars
- **A weekly routine** (15 min) where you scan what's come in and save anything important to Zotero
